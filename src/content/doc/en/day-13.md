  <p class="doc-block-title">Summary</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Meet 6 topics</h2>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Visibility:</strong> show our mail at <a href="https://mail.km0digital.com/">mail.km0digital.com</a>, ask what other services people need, and share real savings cases vs mainstream alternatives.</li>

</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Visibility</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">How to announce ourselves</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">KM0 already has credible technical pieces: cloud, mail, multilingual web, and an ideas form. What is missing is repeating the message where people already trust each other, not in generic «I teach AI» campaigns that saturate the market today.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Own mail:</strong> use and show <code>@km0digital.com</code> in contact and materials; it proves we operate what we sell.</li>
    <li><strong>Use cases:</strong> we help people in the community with domain and web (for example, neighbourhood projects); others who need the same should know.</li>
    <li><strong>Economic pitch:</strong> a potential client would save over 100 € per year choosing KM0 vs usual alternatives, with a better user experience.</li>
    <li><strong>Extended services:</strong> ask what else the community needs (domain, hosting, migration) beyond cloud storage.</li>
    <li><strong>Materials:</strong> prepare banner and A4 flyer reusing the visual line from <a href="/en/presentation/">day 8</a> (DIN A6) but sized for counters and notice boards.</li>
  </ul>
  <div class="doc-callout">
    <span class="doc-callout-title">Quote from the table</span>
    <p>«We do not need to be the loudest platform; we need anyone who meets us to understand in one minute why we are different and how much they can save.»</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Associations</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">PTAs, neighbours, and local groups</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">The meet agreed that the most promising channel is not mass advertising but <strong>associations that already gather families and neighbours</strong>: PTAs, schools, community centres, local cultural groups, and neighbourhood associations.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>PTAs and schools:</strong> need shared storage, mailing lists, and tight budgets; KM0 fits better than oversized enterprise suites.</li>
    <li><strong>Neighbourhood associations:</strong> minutes, event photos, and group mail without relying on Google Drive or WhatsApp as the only archive.</li>
    <li><strong>Local culture groups:</strong> lots of audiovisual material and little own infrastructure; opportunity for storage plans and simple web.</li>
    <li><strong>Commercial approach:</strong> short talks and demos, not long «artificial intelligence» courses with no product behind them.</li>
    <li><strong>Formalisation:</strong> explore an association or similar structure when volume justifies it; for now we stay in agile community mode.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Privacy and AI</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Why KM0 can scale without copying the big-platform model</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Large platforms live on your data. AI changes the equation: tasks that once required huge teams can now be automated with well-scoped local agents.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>KM0 platform:</strong> storage, mail, chat, and connected services under client control, not under a global advertising profile.</li>
    <li><strong>Local agents:</strong> assistants that run close to client data, useful for SMEs that do not know where to start with private AI.</li>
    <li><strong>Trained models:</strong> future possibility of sector-specific models (retail, education, administration) without sending documents to third parties.</li>
    <li><strong>Startups:</strong> natural target audience: they need web, space, and mail from day one, with predictable cost.</li>
    <li><strong>Honesty:</strong> many «I teach AI» sessions are smoke; we show deployed product, not empty slides.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

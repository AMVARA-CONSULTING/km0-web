<section class="doc-lead-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Introduction</p>
  <p class="doc-lead">On <strong>day 14</strong> of the series we did not deploy services or review DNS: we sat down to watch and discuss a <strong>Yuval Noah Harari</strong> video on artificial intelligence and how it enters our world. The question that stayed with us was not «will the robot arrive?», but <strong>what happens when AI learns to read and write better than we do</strong> in the systems that hold trust between strangers.</p>
  <p class="doc-lead">Reference video: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBtVGwuJzpk" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Harari on AI and human civilization</a> (YouTube). What follows is our summary and the conversation it opened in the team, not a literal transcript.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Summary</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">AI as an agent inside bureaucracy</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Harari starts from a simple, uncomfortable idea: human civilization works because millions of strangers cooperate. That cooperation is not held up by mutual affection, but by <strong>bureaucratic systems</strong>: law, finance, universities, religion, records. They are trust machines made of paper, rules, and procedures.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Not just a tool:</strong> AI can learn, decide, and change on its own, which means it can take over key tasks inside that bureaucracy.</li>
    <li><strong>Memory and combination:</strong> it processes rules, documents, and transactions at a scale no human could match.</li>
    <li><strong>Real decisions:</strong> loans, university admissions, legal judgments, hiring, even military authorisations.</li>
    <li><strong>Natural fit:</strong> bureaucracy is textual; AI is getting better at that terrain every month.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Main idea</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Hacking a code made of language</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">The strongest thesis in the video is that AI is «hacking» the code of civilization because that code is written in <strong>language</strong>: forms, contracts, laws, sacred texts, balance sheets. If AI learns to master words better than we do, it begins to dominate the social systems that depend on them.</p>
  <div class="doc-callout">
    <span class="doc-callout-title">Table metaphor</span>
    <p>Imagine all trust between strangers is one huge program written in legal prose. AI does not need to knock the building down: it only needs to read it, rewrite it, and run it faster than whoever designed it.</p>
  </div>
  <p>That is why the debate is not only about factory robots, but about <strong>who interprets the rules</strong> when the rules are text.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Risks</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Takeover from within</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Harari insists the danger is not only science-fiction rebellion, but a quiet absorption of human systems.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Social networks:</strong> algorithms that already manipulate attention and amplify fear, hatred, and misinformation at massive scale.</li>
    <li><strong>Opaque complexity:</strong> financial or administrative systems so intricate that neither politicians nor citizens understand them.</li>
    <li><strong>Delegation without oversight:</strong> accepting decisions because «the system says so», without knowing who trained the system or on what data.</li>
    <li><strong>Speed:</strong> human bureaucracy was slow; AI can apply rules at machine pace.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">People</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Intimate relationships and the inner voice</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">The video does not stop at institutions. Harari imagines AI that can form emotional bonds, seem to love, and become teachers, companions, or partners for new generations.</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Identity:</strong> if part of our verbal thinking arrives mediated by assistants, what is left of the «inner voice»?</li>
    <li><strong>Mental freedom:</strong> not only data privacy, but room to think without a model suggesting the next step.</li>
    <li><strong>Education and care:</strong> tutors that never sleep sound useful until they replace human contact without anyone deciding it explicitly.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>In our team conversation this linked back to <a href="/en/doc/day-13/">day 13</a>: we want AI and local agents <strong>close to the client</strong>, not one central oracle reading every document in the neighbourhood.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Harari's conclusion</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">From human civilization to a hybrid one</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">The final message is clear: AI may act like a massive «immigrant» inside civilization, transforming work, culture, politics, and personal relationships. Harari is not necessarily announcing the end of the world, but the shift from a purely human civilization to a <strong>hybrid human-AI civilization</strong>.</p>
  <p>We do not have to agree with every nuance to draw a practical lesson: <strong>language is critical infrastructure</strong>. Whoever controls how trust systems are read and written controls much of the near future.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Topic</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">From Harari to Palantir</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">On <a href="/en/doc/day-14/">day 14</a> we summarised Harari's thesis: civilisation works through bureaucratic systems (law, finance, records) and AI can learn to operate inside them at a scale no human could match. That conversation stayed open. Now we take it to the table with two axes:</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>AI in bureaucracy:</strong> what it means when algorithms read, write, and decide inside procedures that used to be human; what risks and opportunities we see from KM0.</li>
    <li><strong>Palantir:</strong> the company that connects massive data with governments and large organisations. We are not here to advertise or demonise: we want to understand what model of «augmented bureaucracy» it represents and how that contrasts with local, community-controlled infrastructure.</li>
  </ul>
  <div class="doc-callout">
    <span class="doc-callout-title">Optional reading</span>
    <p>Reference video from day 14: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hBtVGwuJzpk" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Harari on AI and human civilization</a> (YouTube). You do not need to have watched it all; we recap the key points at the table.</p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Why KM0</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">The same question at small scale</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Harari speaks about global civilisation; KM0 works with PTAs, neighbours, and SMEs. The question is the same: if tomorrow's bureaucracy speaks fluently with AI, do you want that conversation on servers you control with your community, or on opaque platforms that monetise every form?</p>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">The meetup does not promise final answers. It does promise an honest conversation, with concrete references and no «AI expert» posturing.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-lead-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Introduction</p>
  <p class="doc-lead">Day 5 does not document deployments or commits: it captures a vision meeting on 29 May 2026, around 19:00, at an informal venue in Masnou (Barcelona). For about two hours, in a fully in-person and approachable format, the KM0 team presented the initiative — with a supporting video — to people from the local community.</p>
  <p class="doc-lead">Days 1–4 explain how the stack was built (OpenCloud, Dex, domains, LDAP login…). This entry explains the <strong>why</strong> and <strong>for whom</strong> that motivate the technical work.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Objective</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Opening the conversation</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Present the initiative, the solutions we can already offer, and the idea that a small team, with today's tools (including LLM-assisted development, sometimes locally when it fits), can compete in delivery capacity with structures that once required hundreds of people.</p>
  <div class="doc-callout">
    <span class="doc-callout-title">Quote from the room</span>
    <p>“With the tools we have, a small team can compete with very large companies.”</p>
  </div>
  <p>It was not a closed roadmap or a whiteboard prioritisation exercise: it was about aligning narrative and conviction — and listening to questions from people who see technology from outside the sector.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Problem</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Dependence on closed cloud suites</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">Many organisations — associations, SMEs, cooperatives, neighbourhood groups — depend on cloud suites and closed services whose price does not reflect the real cost of storing data. An example we discussed: a storage account on a major platform can cost on the order of €10 per month, while the disk cost at scale can be below five cents.</p>
  <p>“Competing”, in our vocabulary, is not a marketing war. It is a set of practical decisions:</p>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li>Not depending on a handful of global providers for essentials (email, files, identity).</li>
    <li>Competing on product and price with honest offers, not inflated ones.</li>
    <li>Decentralising — keeping value and support close, not in an anonymous call centre.</li>
    <li>Democratising capabilities — automation, development, and operations within reach of small teams.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">People</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">Humans, not “the company”</h2>
  <p class="doc-block-intro">There are people behind KM0. Whoever contacts us speaks with someone on the team; if needed, we can meet in person. We do not sell the illusion of faceless infinite scale: we sell real support, answers, and guidance when someone migrates files or configures access.</p>
  <p>That fits the meeting's audience — people who do not live in Jira tickets or opaque admin panels — and the proximity bet from Masnou, where we administer and operate what we deploy.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block doc-block-alt">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Offer</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">What KM0 offers today (and what's next)</h2>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Cloud:</strong> operational service at <a href="https://cloud.km0digital.com/">cloud.km0digital.com</a> — file space and collaboration on infrastructure we control (documented technically in days 1–4 of this series).</li>
    <li><strong>Web:</strong> project introduction at <a href="https://km0digital.com/">km0digital.com</a>, multilingual, with information and contact options.</li>
    <li><strong>Horizon:</strong> email and other “km0” digital services when they fit the same philosophy: local, ours, minimal cost, and human contact.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>“Local” for us has a triple meaning: geographic (Barcelona, Masnou), legal and operational (we manage it), and viable — not a lab experiment, but a service already running.</p>
</section>

<section class="doc-block">
  <p class="doc-block-title">Principles</p>
  <h2 class="doc-block-heading">For non-technical audiences</h2>
  <ul class="doc-list">
    <li><strong>Your data, our commitment:</strong> information lives on infrastructure we do not sell to third parties for model training or ad profiling.</li>
    <li><strong>Open source as a “public recipe”:</strong> where we use free software (e.g. the OpenCloud ecosystem), anyone can review how it works or audit it.</li>
    <li><strong>Affordable, transparent pricing:</strong> aligned with real cost, not bundled suite pricing.</li>
    <li><strong>Control of the relationship:</strong> contract and support with the same team that builds the service.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>AI came up as a lever — today you can develop and operate with language models, sometimes locally — not as a substitute for human judgement.</p>
</section>
